About

I am a Principal AI & Cybersecurity Architect focused on building AI-first threat intelligence and security platforms that operate reliably at scale.

My work sits at the intersection of cyber threat research, machine learning, and production system design. I design end-to-end pipelines that turn large volumes of unstructured cyber data into high-confidence, structured intelligence that can be trusted by analysts, automated systems, and downstream products.

I operate as a senior individual contributor, owning systems from initial design through production deployment and long-term evolution. My role typically involves defining technical direction, making architectural trade-offs, and translating research-grade ideas into systems that can run continuously, predictably, and cost-effectively.

How I work

My approach is shaped by production constraints rather than demos.

  • I design systems from the data model outward, not from the UI inward.
  • I favor precision, traceability, and explainability over raw recall when outcomes matter.
  • I treat AI components as fallible subsystems that require guardrails, evaluation, and monitoring.
  • I optimize for long-term maintainability, not short-term velocity.
  • I assume systems will scale, fail, and be misused — and design accordingly.

I am comfortable operating in ambiguity, but I work toward clear interfaces, explicit assumptions, and measurable behavior.

What I build

Most of my work involves systems such as:

  • AI-driven threat intelligence enrichment pipelines
  • Agentic LLM systems and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)
  • Entity extraction and normalization at scale
  • Automation layers that bridge research, data, and production
  • Platforms that balance accuracy, cost, and operational reliability

I am particularly interested in the practical limits of AI systems — where heuristics, rules, and human judgment still matter, and how to compose them effectively with machine learning.

What I don’t optimize for

At this stage of my career, there are things I deliberately avoid:

  • I don’t build AI demos that cannot be productionized.
  • I don’t optimize for hype or novelty at the expense of correctness.
  • I don’t treat large language models as magic components.
  • I don’t design systems that only work under ideal conditions.

I prefer fewer systems that work predictably over many systems that work occasionally.

Scope

I typically work on problems that require:

  • System-level thinking across multiple components
  • Deep understanding of failure modes and trade-offs
  • Close collaboration with research, product, and security stakeholders
  • Long-term ownership rather than short-term delivery

I am most effective in environments that value technical depth, clarity of thought, and engineering judgment.

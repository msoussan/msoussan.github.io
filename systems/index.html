Systems

AI-Driven Threat Intelligence Enrichment Platform

Role: Principal Architect / Senior Individual Contributor

Context

Modern cyber threat intelligence pipelines ingest large volumes of unstructured data: news articles, technical blogs, advisories, incident reports, and open-source intelligence. The primary challenge is not collection, but reliable transformation — converting noisy, heterogeneous text into structured intelligence that can be trusted by analysts and automated systems.

This system was designed to address scale, precision, and operational reliability simultaneously, while remaining adaptable to evolving threat landscapes.

Architecture

  • Ingestion: Continuous ingestion of heterogeneous text sources with normalization and deduplication.
  • Orchestration: Pipeline-driven execution with explicit stages, retries, and failure isolation.
  • AI & NLP Layer: Named Entity Recognition (NER) and classification for threat actors, malware, victims, and campaigns.
  • Enrichment: Contextual augmentation, cross-referencing, and confidence scoring.
  • Storage: Structured data models optimized for downstream querying and analysis.
  • Consumers: Analyst-facing tools and automated detection systems.

Key Decisions

  • Prioritized precision and traceability over raw recall to reduce analyst fatigue.
  • Used AI components as assistive subsystems, not authoritative sources.
  • Separated orchestration from enrichment logic to enable independent evolution.
  • Designed explicit schemas to prevent silent semantic drift in extracted entities.
  • Built cost-awareness and evaluation into the pipeline rather than treating them as afterthoughts.

Outcomes

  • Significant reduction in manual analyst effort for routine enrichment tasks.
  • Improved consistency and quality of structured intelligence.
  • Increased reliability of downstream automation relying on enriched data.
  • A platform capable of evolving with new AI techniques without destabilizing production workflows.

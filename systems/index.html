CASE_STUDY_01

AI-First Cyber Threat Intelligence Pipeline

Discuss Design
Broad Source Coverage Continuous intake across external reporting, advisories, and intelligence feeds.
Structured Processing Model Collection, enrichment, normalization, clustering, and delivery are kept explicitly separated.
Fast Turnaround Built for quick end-to-end refresh without giving up operational control.
Modular Service Design Responsibilities are separated so reliability, maintenance, and cost can be managed deliberately.
Hybrid Data Layer Search, storage, and serving are designed to support both enrichment workflows and analyst consumption.

Problem

Cybersecurity teams operate on top of a constant stream of unstructured data — RSS feeds, vendor reports, and external intelligence sources. Raw data is noisy, duplicated, and inconsistent. Generic summarization loses the entities and relationships that make intelligence actionable. The challenge is not collecting data — it is turning it into something structured, reliable, and usable at scale.

Approach

I designed an end-to-end pipeline that ingests broad external coverage, rejects noise early, and enforces structure through schema-first extraction. Collection, enrichment, normalization, and serving are separated on purpose so the system remains observable, maintainable, and cost-aware in production.

Key Design Decisions

  • Early rejection filters irrelevant data before expensive AI processing starts.
  • Schema-first extraction reduces ambiguity and avoids brittle post-processing cleanup.
  • Entity normalization keeps actors, malware, vulnerabilities, and other signals consistent across sources.
  • Clustering and temporal linking reconstruct evolving narratives instead of leaving analysts with isolated articles.
  • Multi-signal scoring prioritizes the stories and entities that matter operationally.

Outcome

The system turns raw reporting into structured intelligence that analysts can follow as evolving stories rather than isolated articles. It supports tracking campaigns, vulnerabilities, and actors over time while keeping reliability and operational control in view.

CASE_STUDY_02

Agentic CTI Investigator

Discuss Design
Focused Specialist Reasoning Targeted timeline, correlation, comparison, and trend analysis when the question demands it.
Measured Quality Discipline Internal evaluation is used to check grounding, routing quality, and answer robustness.
Efficient Repeat Investigations Reuse and caching reduce repeated analysis cost and turnaround for common investigation patterns.
Grounded Answer Quality Designed for high grounded-answer precision rather than generic chatbot fluency.

Problem

Analysts do not need a chatbot — they need an investigation system. Questions require context, follow-ups, comparisons, and evidence-backed answers. A single prompt cannot manage retrieval, reasoning, and validation reliably.

Approach

I designed a multi-agent investigation system where orchestration, retrieval, specialist reasoning, and verification are separated into explicit layers. Depending on the question, it can take a direct retrieval path, a structured analysis path, or a deeper specialist path while staying grounded in evidence.

Key Design Decisions

  • Adaptive routing lets the system match its execution path to the actual question type.
  • Live context injection keeps reasoning aligned with the current data model rather than a static prompt snapshot.
  • Specialist agents add targeted reasoning for timeline, correlation, comparison, and trend questions.
  • Layered verification combines model review with deterministic checks so answers stay grounded.
  • Controlled revision loops improve reliability when retrieval or grounding issues are detected.

Outcome

The system produces grounded answers, structured reports, and proactive monitoring outputs backed by explicit evidence. It behaves less like a chatbot and more like a controlled investigation workflow.
